ANALYTICAL APPROACH FOR FLOW PAST CYLINDER

Fig. 1.
Cylinder in a freestream Streamline pattern around unit radiouse cylinder.

In analytical method, complex velocity potential of cylinder in a free stream (Fig. 1) is defined according to Milne-Thomson circle theorem which was mentioned in Eqs. 1.

w(z) = f(z)+f ̅(a^2/z)= f (z)+f ̅(z)-------------------- (1)

w (z) can be also expressed as in equation 2. a indicates the radius of the circle.

w (z)= 〖-V_∞ e〗^(-iα)-V_∞ (a^2/z) e^iα-------------------- (2)

Complex velocity is obtained by differentiating equation 2 which has two components of velocities.α indicate angle of attack.

Complex velocity =( V) ̅=〖V_∞ e〗^(-iα) (e^2iα-e^(-2iϑ) )------------------ (3)

Tangential velocity is obtained using equation 3.If the tangent velocity and freestream velocity are known, pressure coefficient can be obtained by Eqs 4.

C_(p,i )=1-(V_i/V_∞ )^2 ---------------------(4)

V_i,V_∞ indicate tangential velocity and freestream velocity respectively

PANEL METHOD APPROACH FOR FLOW PAST CYLINDER

Panel method can be applied for a cylindrical shape, in the same way of applying for arbitrary shape to find the pressure flow parameters. Surface of the cylinder is divided into panels. Midpoint of the panel is called control point or collocation point. Actual shape of the obstacle can be obtained by increasing the number of panels.
